Teaching Philosophy

Teaching Philosophy

My ideal school is that a community of learners working in a warm, positive, friendly and engaging environment where all members proudly display the values of respect, integrity, responsibility, consideration and enjoyment. In order to produce happy, competent, resilient community minded individuals who are able to live productive lives.

Have the content knowledge and pedagogical practice to meet the diverse needs of all students

Provide expert advice about the content, processes and strategies that will shape individual and school professional learning.

Model exemplary classroom practice and mentor/coach other teachers in the school to engage in critical reflection of their practice and to support staff to expand their capacity.

Demonstrate capacity to run an engaging and high quality Chinese language program for secondary students.

Demonstrate ability to monitor and assess student learning data and to sue this data to inform teaching for improved student learning.

Demonstrate high level written and verbal communication skills and high level interpersonal skills including the ability to develop constructive relationships with staff, parents and students.

Classroom teachers play a significant role in assisting the school to improve student performance and educational outcomes determined by the school strategic plan and state-wide priorities and contributing to the development and implementation of school policies and priorities.

Demonstrate commitment and ability to actively contribute to a broad range of activities and a commitment to building and fostering an inclusive learning community.

A critical component of teaching language will focus on increasing the micro-skills including spoken, listening, reading and writing skills about improving students’ communication skills by using another language and high quality instruction combining effective teaching materials to assist students’ learning to define quality teacher practice.
